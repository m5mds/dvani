# Divani UI/UX Audit And Visual Direction

## Executive Diagnosis

The current website is not failing because it lacks polish. It is failing because it is trying to look premium through styling instead of through a clear creative direction.

Right now the page has dramatic colors, overlays, reveals, chapter words, bordered panels, hover states, and gold accents. But the emotional read is still: decorated presentation page. For an interior design / fit-out studio, the desired read should be: expensive spatial experience.

The work itself should be the luxury object. The interface should almost disappear.

## What Is Not Working

1. The page is too UI-heavy.
   The current layout has too many boxes, labels, panels, counters, borders, and widget-like elements. It feels designed as a web interface, not as a portfolio for physical spaces.

2. The imagery is under-commanding.
   Divani's strongest asset is project photography, especially the rattan installation, auditoriums, hotel interiors, and material details. The current page uses images, but often inside crops, cards, or overlays that reduce their power.

3. The dark/gold palette is becoming a cliche.
   Black plus gold can feel premium, but when used everywhere it quickly becomes generic luxury. The brand needs warmer material contrast: walnut, bone, shadow, glass, muted steel, soft champagne, and controlled black.

4. The cream sections feel like presentation slides.
   They create contrast, but they also break the cinematic mood. The large empty cream blocks feel calm, not seductive. They should either become gallery-white editorial pages with excellent spacing, or be replaced with image-led sections.

5. The mobile experience stacks too much.
   On mobile, every section becomes a tall column of blocks. It is clear, but not sexy. Mobile needs fewer components, larger image moments, and stronger breathing rhythm.

6. The service/process content still feels administrative.
   A client does need clarity, but not in the emotional core of the page. Services and process should be quieter, almost like captions in a gallery, while the projects do the selling.

## Better Creative Direction

### Direction Name

**The Cinematic Spatial Lookbook**

### Mood

Quiet, sensual, architectural, dimly lit, material-rich, confident. More boutique hotel lobby than corporate company profile. More editorial fashion campaign than contractor brochure.

### Design Principle

Do not decorate the website. Stage the spaces.

The visitor should feel:

- "These people understand atmosphere."
- "Their work looks real and expensive."
- "I can trust them because they have completed serious spaces."
- "I know how to start without reading a long profile."

## Proposed Page Structure

### 1. Opening: Full-Screen Signature Moment

Use one dominant image only. Prefer the rattan installation or the strongest hospitality shot.

Recommended treatment:

- Full-bleed image, nearly edge to edge.
- Minimal nav.
- Logo small and quiet.
- One strong Arabic headline.
- One short sentence.
- Two CTAs maximum: WhatsApp and View Work.
- Remove stats from the hero. Put credibility later.

Target feeling: "I just entered the space."

### 2. Project Runway

Replace the current project selector with a seductive project runway.

Recommended treatment:

- Large vertical or horizontal project slides.
- Each project gets one hero image, one title, one location/client line, and one short phrase.
- Use image transitions that feel like a camera cut, not a UI carousel.
- Add project thumbnails only as a slim filmstrip or index.

Target feeling: "This is a portfolio worth exploring."

### 3. Material Close-Ups

Keep the material idea, but make it more tactile and less like cards.

Recommended treatment:

- Huge close-up crops: rattan, ceiling light, wall panel, stone/glass.
- Use captions as small gallery labels.
- Add one "material language" sentence, not four explanatory blocks.

Target feeling: "They notice detail."

### 4. Proof Through Restraint

Credentials should be quiet and precise.

Recommended treatment:

- Since 2009.
- Interior design, fit-out, material supply, furnishing.
- Hotels, lounges, halls, commercial spaces.
- Keep this as a thin horizontal proof band or understated typographic section.

Target feeling: "Established, not loud."

### 5. Services As A Calm Menu

Services should not compete visually with projects.

Recommended treatment:

- Four service lines, no big cards.
- Each line opens or expands lightly if needed.
- Use verbs: Design, Build, Supply, Furnish.
- Arabic wording should be simple and client-friendly.

Target feeling: "They can handle the whole thing."

### 6. Start Section

The final contact section can stay direct, but it needs to feel like a private invitation.

Recommended treatment:

- Full-bleed interior image.
- Large short headline.
- WhatsApp primary action.
- Phone and profile link secondary.
- Remove excess table/grid energy.

Target feeling: "Send them the space now."

## Visual System

### Palette

- Obsidian black: primary background.
- Warm bone: text and quiet editorial pages.
- Soft champagne: single accent, used sparingly.
- Walnut brown: image-inspired warmth.
- Muted steel/green: secondary material accent.

Avoid using gold as a constant button/border color. Gold should appear like jewelry: small, deliberate, rare.

### Typography

Use stronger contrast:

- Large Arabic display headlines with generous line-height and no cramped decorative effects.
- Small uppercase English only for labels like "PROJECT", "MATERIAL", "FIT-OUT".
- Body copy should be smaller, calmer, and less frequent.

The current type scale is dramatic, but too many sections shout. Only the hero and project moments should be loud.

### Layout

Move away from repeated section cards.

Use:

- Full-bleed image scenes.
- Thin gallery captions.
- Asymmetric editorial grids.
- Large negative space where the image is strong enough.
- Fewer borders and fewer panels.

### Motion

Motion should feel like camera movement:

- Slow image fade.
- Slight parallax on hero.
- Project transition like a lens cut.
- No excessive reveal choreography.

## What I Would Remove

- The giant background chapter words in every section.
- Most bordered boxes.
- The current cream service block layout.
- The dashboard-like project list.
- Repeated labels and counters.
- Any decorative treatment that does not directly make the space/image feel more desirable.

## What I Would Keep

- The Arabic-first direction.
- The simplified client-friendly copy.
- The WhatsApp-first conversion path.
- The rattan hero asset.
- The idea of project proof.
- The idea of material close-ups.

## Implementation Plan

1. Re-extract stronger project imagery from the PDF at higher resolution.
2. Choose 6-8 hero-worthy images and 6-8 detail crops.
3. Redesign the page as an image-led lookbook with fewer components.
4. Replace service cards with a quiet service menu.
5. Replace the current project selector with a cinematic project runway.
6. Rework mobile first: image, headline, CTA, project image, proof, WhatsApp.
7. Verify with screenshots at desktop and mobile before tuning effects.

## Success Criteria

The redesigned site is successful if a first-time viewer can say, within 5 seconds:

- This is an interior design / fit-out studio.
- Their work looks premium and real.
- They have completed serious projects.
- I know how to contact them.

And emotionally:

- The page feels quieter.
- The images feel larger.
- The brand feels more expensive.
- The interface feels less present.
